GROWING UP

The majority of my childhood was spent in Muskegon, MI. Situated along the eastern coast of Lake Michigan, about half-way up the Lower Peninsula coast, I spend a lot of time out exploring the countless dune fields that line the Lake Michigan coast. Although my family moved to West Michigan early on, I have maintained strong ties to several farming communities between Lansing and Jackson, MI. Growing up around and helping on farms throughout the years has helped me understand a variety of landscapes, which helps in interpreting landscapes from aerial and satellite images.

In April of 2008 I became a certified SCUBA diver. I presently hold SCUBA Schools International (SSI) certification as an Open Water Diver and Dry Suit Diver. BACKPACKING IN THE HIMALAYAS

Camping and backpacking has always been am important part of my life. I'm comfortable in a wide variety of adverse conditions. Although a majority of my backpacking and camping experiences have been in the State of Michigan, though in the summer 2008 I participated in a MSU Study Abroad program through the College of Agriculture and Natural Resources: Ecology of the Mountains. The program ran about 5 weeks, 3 weeks of which were spend trekking in the Himalayan Mountains of Uttaranchal, India. We traveled several horizontal kilometers a day over very rough trails. The trek peaked at the base of two different glaciers (Pindari and Kafni), where we set up camp for several nights. Camping at about 12,000 ft above sea level (asl) and peaking at 13,000 ft asl one morning, it was a tremendous experience that I would not trade for anything. Upon completion of the trekking portion of the trip we traveled via train to visit the Agra Fort and Taj Mahal.

After spending a majority of the summer after graduation from MSU job searching and beach-going at Pere Marquette I have determined that this will be the next sport I pick up. It is directly due to my interest and involvement that I find problem solving a challenge to be met and never shied away from. This is quite possibly the reason my MSU undergrad graduation gift from my parents was my dream tractor of an Oliver 2150 FWA.
